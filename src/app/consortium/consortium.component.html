Project Consortium

Logical characterization of awareness in robots through symbolic methods: Creating a logic-based framework for robots with internal awareness, collaborating with humans. A new symbolic temporal logic for MASs includes "knowledge" propositions and awareness operators, capturing relative conceptual awareness between robots. The logic encodes situational awareness in MASs, enabling automated logic-founded reasoning and verification, implemented in an open-source computational toolbox.

Team Leader: Dr. Sadegh Soudjani

Spatio-temporal awareness in task planning: The next step is quantifying symbolic reasoning for awareness (from the first Objective) with spatial and temporal elements. Spatio-temporal logics model awareness, defining quantifiable space and time matrices for system tasks. Using these matrices,to create spatio temporal awareness as decision-making factors in single and multi-agent task planning. Address complexity and scalability in spatio temporal awareness-based decision-making with decentralized solutions.

Team Leader: Prof. Dimos V. Dimarogonas

Risk awareness via quantified knowledge: Agents (robots and humans) will gain awareness of individual and multi-agent risks. This requires extending risk quantification and awareness to agents' symbolic reasoning in uncertain environments and developing methods to communicate risk to humans. Risks are differentiated based on quantifiable likelihood (predictable uncertainty) and unforeseen changes, ensuring safety in the system by quantifying both risk types based on available knowledge for each agent.

Team Leader: Dr. Sofie Haesaert

Quantifying and communicating knowledge awareness: We aim to enhance human-robot interaction through social awareness, transparency, and knowledge abstraction, fostering sustainable autonomy and communication between robots and humans. Identify requirements for ethical and trustworthy awareness in human-agent interaction: We aim to develop user-centered design methods for trustworthy human-agent interaction in MASs, addressing ethical aspects, safety, fairness, and bias prevention.

Team Leader: Prof. Ginevra Castellano

Demonstrate awareness engineering in aviation: The SymAware approach for awareness engineering will be implemented and validated in two use cases. A first use case concerns modelling, simulation and risk assessment of unmanned aircraft systems traffic management (UTM) for drone operations in an urban environment, including disturbances and hazards during operations

Team Leader: Dr. Sybert Stroeve

Demonstrate awareness engineering in automotive use cases: A second use case uses the SymAware methods for simulation and analysis of higher levels of autonomous vehicles, assuring functional safety (due to failures) as well as Safety of the Intended Functionality (SOTIF). Both use cases will evaluate the effectiveness of various awareness strategies and their implications for design.

Team Leader: Dr. Mohsen Alirezaei
