SUSTAINABLE

AUTONOMY

Picture an environment where autonomous agents (robots, drones, cars) collaborate with humans fulfilling complex and dynamic changing tasks. To have real, sustainable autonomy, these agents need to obtain and improve adequate situational awareness and actively perceive risks.

This is the purpose of Symbolic Logic Framework for Situational Awareness in Mixed Autonomy (SymAware), a project funded by the European Union.

Project Consortium

Max Planck Institute for Software Systems (MPI-SWS) studies the principles of efficient, dependable, secure, and usable computing systems, as well as their interaction with the physical and social context in which they operate. The Institute conducts foundational research in relevant areas of computer science and beyond, covering theory, empirical analysis, and data-driven investigation.

Personnel: Dr. Sadegh Soudjani

It works in close collaboration with other universities, scientific institutes, social organizations, government, and industry.

Kungliga Tekniska Högskolan (KTH) provides one-third of Sweden’s capacity for engineering studies and technical research at post-secondary level.
